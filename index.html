04
Sacred Architecture
Sacred & Community Architecture
Mosques · Community centers · Cultural sensitivity · New York & international
+
Description

Sacred architecture demands something that most technical briefs cannot quantify: the ability to listen to a community, understand its traditions, and translate both into form, light, and space. Over 15 years, I've designed prayer halls, minarets, wudu facilities, educational wings, and community gathering spaces for communities across New York — and internationally. Each project begins not with a design, but with a conversation.

Services
  • Community Engagement Process — facilitated stakeholder sessions to ensure every design decision reflects the community's vision and values
  • Cultural & Religious Programming — space planning that honors ritual requirements, acoustic needs, and gender separation with elegance
  • Prayer Hall Design — qibla orientation, muqarnas-inspired detailing, natural light strategy, and acoustic tuning
  • Minaret & Dome Design — structurally engineered landmark elements designed in authentic dialogue with tradition
  • Wudu Facility Design — code-compliant, dignified ablution spaces integrated seamlessly into the building program
  • Acoustic Design — engineered reverberation, sound distribution, and amplification system coordination
  • Full Permit Coordination — NYC DOB, local zoning boards, and landmark review where applicable
  • ADA Compliance — full accessibility integration without compromise to programmatic integrity
